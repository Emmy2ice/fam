THE ORIGIN OF THE AQUARIUS

February was the eleventh month to be named in the calendar year. It was added by Numa Pompilius about 713 BC and was the last month of the year until 450 BC when it was then moved to be the second month of the calendar year.

The name 'February' comes from the Roman festival of purification called 'Februa'. During this festival, people were ritually washed. It would interest you to know that February used to be called the 'cabbage month'. Perhaps, it was so called because the medieval English were eating lots of cabbage in February(!).

Did you know that North Korea and Cuba are the only countries where you can't buy Coca Cola? It's something that most people usually take for granted - that whereever you go, you could always enjoy a bottle of Coke. Well, news alert! You can't enjoy one in North Korea or Cuba.

HEALTH AND LIFESTYLE

VARICOSE VEINS
(SPIDER VEINS)

Not many of us, if any, would be familiar with varicose veins. Varicose veins which are also called varicoses or varicosities (healthline.com, 2019), are simply twisted and enlarged veins which have been overfilled with blood and it can happen in superficial veins (i.e veins near the surface of the skin) anywhere in the body but are more common in the legs (Johns Hopkins Medicine, 2023). This condition is brought about due to obstruction of blood flow, causing an inability of the blood to flow effectively in the affected area(s). Blood travels in one direction in the veins and loss of flexibility and/or elasticity due to stretching of the veins may cause the valves of the veins to get weaker. This can cause blood to flow backwards in opposite direction. When this happens, blood can accumulate in vein(s) which then become enlarged and swollen. Veins farthest from the heart, such as those in the legs, are the most often affected ones. This is because gravity makes it harder for the blood to flow back to the heart (MedicalNewsToday, 2022).

It is very common, especially in women (healthline.com, 2019). An estimated 23% of adults are thought to be affected by varicose veins and approximately 1 in 4 adults in the United States suffer this condition (MedicalNewsToday, 2022). However, Varicoses is not considered a serious medical condition (Johns Hopkins Medicine, 2023).

    Risk Factors
  • Pregnancy
  • Menopause
  • Age (over 50 years)
  • Standing for long periods of time
  • Obesity
  • Family history of varicoses
    • (healthline.com, 2019)

Because it is not a serious medical condition, doctors are usually conservative when treating varicoses. Instead of undergoing more aggressive treatments, patients are rather encouraged to make lifestyle changes like avoiding standing for extended periods of time, losing weight or maintaining a healthy weight, exercising to improve blood circulation, use of compression socks or stockings (healthline.com, 2019). However, if lifestyle changes are not working and the patient is experiencing pain or needs the varicose veins to be removed for aesthetic reasons, a surgery may be required (MedicalNewsToday, 2022).
